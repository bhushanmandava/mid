About

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He is best known for his theory of relativity and his famous equation E=mc², which describes the relationship between mass and energy. Einstein’s work revolutionized our understanding of the universe and paved the way for many technological advancements. He was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.
